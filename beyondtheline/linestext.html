"Demography is destiny." Or is it?

The 2016 U.S. Presidential election sparked significant reconsideration of the relationship between demographics and voting patterns in the United States. In the weeks after the election, media outlets' attribution of the election's results to an overlooked "silent majority" invalidated long-held narratives that saw the continued increases in the propotion of non-white and college-educated Americans as a guarantor of victory for the Democratic party.
