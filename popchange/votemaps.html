About the project >>

The purpose of this work is two-fold. At one level, the project explores that distortions produced by spatial abstractions that underlie both the administration of the state and maps themselves as a form of diagram. At a second level, the project allows the user to construct new knowledge of the contemporary relationship between election results and demographics, using the state of Pennsylvania as a case study.

These goals are pursued through the decomposition of conventional spatial boundaries, and the subsequent re-agglomeration of their associated data onto different spatial groupings. More concretely, the project operates in two parts: the first presents election data at the level of the census tract, as a means to create a more granular understanding of the distribution of votes that what can be seen at the county level.
