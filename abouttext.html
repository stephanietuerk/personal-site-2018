Hi there. It's me, Stephanie. I care about how knowledge is produced in the world.

I am trained as a historian, an architect, and an engineer. In 2017 I left academia after a postdoc, and today I bring my skills, experience, and interests together through data visualization. This site shows some of my personal work.

I have a wide variety of interests, and enjoy few things more than learning new things, through both reading and other media and direct engagement with the world.

Currently I'm thinking a lot about uncertainty, from technical/mathematical, social, and visualization perspectives. Ian Hacking's The Emergence of Probability and The Taming of Chance, and Ted Porter's Trust in Numbers were formative texts for me in this domain. For visualizations, I love working with the d3 library, and I think it is criminal how little attention is paid to Jacques Bertin's 1967 Sémiologie graphique.

Je parle français und auch deutsch and I have a soft spot for all things Singapore, having taught at Singapore University of Technology and Design for three semesters.

I currently live in Cambridge/Somerville, MA.
